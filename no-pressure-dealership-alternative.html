Why Most People Dread Buying a Car

Most Canadians dread the dealership — and for good reason. High-pressure sales tactics. Confusing pricing. Hours lost waiting while a salesperson "talks to the manager." And the nagging feeling that you're overpaying no matter what you do.

Studies consistently show that car buying is one of the most stressful purchases people make. It's not because the cars are bad — it's because the process is designed to benefit the dealer, not the buyer.

Common Dealership Pressure Tactics

If you've been to a dealership, you've probably experienced some of these:

  • "This price is only good today" — Creates urgency to stop you from comparing deals
  • "Let me talk to my manager" — A scripted routine to make you feel like you're getting a special deal
  • Payment-focused selling — Steering the conversation to monthly payments hides the true price
  • The four-square worksheet — Mixing trade-in, purchase price, down payment, and monthly payment to confuse the numbers
  • F&I office add-ons — After you think the deal is done, a finance manager tries to sell warranties, coatings, and protection packages
  • Holding your keys/trade-in — Taking your trade-in for "appraisal" to keep you at the dealer longer
