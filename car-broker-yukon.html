Car Broker in Yukon

Flat-fee dealer outreach and Alberta-to-Whitehorse sourcing for Yukoners who don't want to compromise on the trim.

Written by the CarBrokerCanada team.

Why Use a Broker in Yukon

Yukon's automotive retail is concentrated in Whitehorse — a handful of new-vehicle franchises plus a long tail of pre-owned and independent dealers. Selection on common trims is workable; depth on specific configurations is thin, and waiting for stock to come in from southern Canada is part of life if you want something specific. Buyers outside Whitehorse face a Whitehorse trip on top of all that.

A car broker compresses the search. We work the Whitehorse dealers, reach into Alberta when local inventory is short, and coordinate transport up the Alaska Highway so the vehicle lands ready for pickup.

How Yukon Tax Works on a Vehicle Purchase

Yukon has no territorial sales tax — only the 5% federal GST. That puts Yukon (along with Alberta and the other two territories) at the bottom of the Canadian tax stack on vehicles. Beyond GST, expect a registration fee through Yukon Motor Vehicles plus dealer documentation fees.

Dealer Coverage and Transport

We work with Whitehorse-area dealers first, then look south when local stock can't deliver:

  • Whitehorse — Yukon's main dealer cluster
  • Alberta sourcing — Edmonton and Grande Prairie dealers frequently hold trims that aren't in Whitehorse, with transport up the Alaska Highway
  • Service to outlying communities — Dawson City, Watson Lake, Haines Junction; the vehicle lands in Whitehorse for pickup

Transport, Folded In

Long-haul transport is part of buying a vehicle in the territory. We get a transport quote up front, fold it into the Deal Summary, and time delivery so registration and pickup line up.
