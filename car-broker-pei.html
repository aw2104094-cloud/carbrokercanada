Car Broker in Prince Edward Island

Flat-fee dealer outreach and negotiation for Charlottetown, Summerside, and across PEI — with mainland sourcing when local inventory is short.

Written by the CarBrokerCanada team.

Why Use a Broker in PEI

PEI is the smallest provincial automotive retail market in Canada. Charlottetown holds most of the franchised dealer count, with Summerside and Montague picking up additional coverage. The catch is depth: PEI dealers carry the brands but rarely the complete trim spread on any given model. A buyer chasing a specific configuration usually needs mainland sourcing.

That's where a broker earns the fee. We work the Island dealers first, then reach into New Brunswick or Nova Scotia when needed, and handle the bridge or ferry transport so you don't have to plan a Confederation Bridge run around inventory.

How PEI Tax Works on a Vehicle Purchase

PEI charges 15% HST on dealer purchases — same headline rate as the other Atlantic provinces. Private sales are taxed differently: a 15% provincial vehicle tax is collected at Access PEI when you register, calculated on the higher of the sale price or the assessed value.

Beyond tax, expect documentation fees at the dealer and registration fees through Access PEI. Every line shows up in the Deal Summary.

Dealer Coverage Across PEI and the Maritimes

We negotiate with dealers in every PEI market and reach into the mainland when local inventory is short:

  • Charlottetown — the Island's main dealer cluster
  • Summerside — west-end coverage
  • Mainland sourcing — Moncton, Halifax, and beyond when a specific trim isn't on the Island

For high-demand vehicles, mainland inventory often beats Island inventory both in availability and pricing. We model the all-in cost (including transport across the Confederation Bridge or via ferry) so the trade-off is clear.

Mainland Transport, Folded In

When the right vehicle is at a mainland dealer, we handle the dealer-to-dealer transfer or arrange direct transport. The transport cost becomes a line in the Deal Summary — no surprise add-ons after you've signed.

Buying Out-of-Province from PEI

Out-of-province sourcing is more common for PEI buyers than almost anywhere else, simply because of the size of the local market. PEI HST applies on registration regardless of where the vehicle was purchased, so the savings have to live in the pre-tax price plus avoiding shipping cost. We model both options in the Deal Summary.
